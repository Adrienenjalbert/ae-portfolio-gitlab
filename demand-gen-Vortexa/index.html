1 Part One · What's actually going on

The numbers are up. The engine is slowing.

Sources 1–3 look like three unrelated notes. Read together they tell one story: the top of the funnel is working harder while the bottom converts worse. Here's each signal, what it says, and how I read it.

Source 1 · Reporting

Volume up, opportunities down

Demo fills +38%, MCLs +16%, SAOs −18% (Q1 vs Q4).

What it says

The funnel got busier at the top and thinner at the bottom. Marketing looks like it's winning on the metrics it owns.

How I read it

Every conversion rate fell. Demo→SAO dropped from 12.8% to 7.5%, roughly −41%. We're buying more of something that converts less. That's a quality problem hiding inside a volume story.

Source 2 · Sales Slack

"A lot of these aren't it"

Students, a consultant doing research. Sales wants it tightened.

What it says

Two true things at once: sales sees bad-fit leads, and the BDR is right that volume is up on an unchanged process.

How I read it

Both are correct, which means the bar is the problem, not the people. The process didn't change, but the mix did. This is the qualitative version of the Source 1 conversion drop, and a relationship I need to repair, not win.

Source 3 · RevOps

Mid-Q2 cleanup. Some "Validate" deals now sit a stage earlier, not restated.

What it says

The pipeline model changed underneath the numbers, and the history wasn't rebased to match.

How I read it

This is the load-bearing caveat. Part of the −18% in SAOs is definitional, not a real decline, deals shifted category, not disappeared. I wouldn't let anyone act on that number until it's rebased. The quality signal is real; the size of it isn't yet known.

1 Part One · Measuring the ABM pilot (Source 4)

Stop counting leads. Measure the account.

The CMO is right: MCLs and SALs mean little across 10–15 named accounts you already know. With a list that small you don't measure volume, you measure movement per account, like a portfolio of named bets.

The reframe

A traditional funnel asks "how many leads?" ABM into named accounts asks "are the accounts we chose getting warmer, wider, and closer to a deal?" The unit of measurement changes from the lead to the account and its buying committee.

Stop reporting
  • MCL / SAL volume
  • Cost per lead
  • Form fills in aggregate
Start reporting
  • Account engagement & committee coverage
  • Target-account pipeline created / influenced
  • Stage progression & velocity vs non-ABM

Being honest about it

You said it's fine if this isn't fully resolved, so here's the real constraint: 10–15 accounts will never be statistically significant. I wouldn't pretend otherwise with a chart.

Instead I'd run it as a scorecard, a clear before/after per account on engagement, committee reach and stage, plus leading indicators the board can see next month while pipeline is still maturing. Judgment, evidenced, not false precision.

2 Part Two · The process I'd AI-augment first

An ABM research & lead-scoring agent.

You have four candidates: lead qualification, the BDR handoff, ABM account research, reporting. I'd automate account research and scoring first, because it's the one process that sits on top of both problems at once, and it's the model that fits "AI-powered workflows, not added headcount".

My pick Option A

ABM research + scoring

Enrich accounts, find the committee, score fit & intent. Fixes quality and powers ABM.

Option B

Lead qualification

Solved as a by-product of scoring, not a separate build.

Option C

BDR handoff

Improves once the leads and briefs it receives are better.

Option D

Reporting

Real, but week-one plumbing, not the highest-leverage agent.

The workflow

How it actually works

Not a vision statement. The steps, the data each one needs, and where a human stays in the loop. I've built the equivalent before, a prospecting agent that enriches accounts, finds decision-makers and personalises outreach at scale.

1
Ingest

Named list + inbound demo fills from CRM (HubSpot / SFDC).

2
Enrich

Firmographics & signals via an enrichment API (Cognism / Clearbit).

3
Score fit

Is this our ICP? Filter out students & consultants against the written bar.

4
Map committee

Find trader, analyst, head of desk, risk, procurement per account.

✓
Human check

Sales reviews fit-score & brief on named accounts before any outreach.

5
Brief & sync

Push a research brief + priority into the CRM & sequence.

Where a human stays in the loop

  • The ICP definition is owned by a human, marketing + sales, not the model. The agent applies the bar; it doesn't set it.
  • Named accounts get eyes. With only 10–15, a BDR reviews every fit-score and brief before outreach goes out.
  • Low-confidence scores route to a human, not to auto-reject. An odd job title shouldn't drop a real buyer.
  • Weekly drift audit on a sample, does the score still match reality as the market moves.

What breaks if I get it wrong

  • False negatives: the agent filters out a genuine trader with a non-standard title. Mitigation: human review of rejects, confidence thresholds.
  • False positives at scale: auto-outreach to bad-fit contacts in a small, tight-knit market damages the brand fast. Mitigation: assisted, not autonomous, at launch.
  • Enrichment garbage-in: stale data scores wrong. Mitigation: freshness checks, source-of-truth in CRM.
  • Sales stops trusting it if early briefs are weak, exactly the relationship I'm trying to fix. Mitigation: start narrow, prove it, then widen.

Live day 8

What I protect

  • Speed to pipeline. Reactivation + capture over a polished new campaign.
  • The named accounts. Human-checked, never auto-blasted.
  • The sales relationship. Better bar, faster follow-up, from day one.

What I do imperfectly, on purpose

  • Attribution: self-reported + directional for now. Precise later.
  • Automation: the agent runs assisted / manual first, automated once trusted.
  • Creative: repurpose existing analyst content, ship rough, don't produce net-new.
  • Demand creation at scale: that's the quarter's job. In two weeks I buy coverage, not brand.

The trade-off is deliberate: in a coverage emergency, a fast, imperfect, measurable motion beats a slow, elegant one. I'd rather show the board real movement in three weeks and refine the measurement behind it than present a perfect plan that hasn't shipped.
