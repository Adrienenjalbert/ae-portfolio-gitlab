A simulation, not a verdict

How to use this without fooling yourself.

A persona is a hypothesis generator. It is grounded in what each market actually did, but it is only ever an input to a real test. These are the rules that keep it honest, the same ones from section 2.4 of the case study.

Never let it confirm a prior

The simulator proposes offers to test. It does not get to bless a decision you already made. A persona that keeps agreeing with you is a red flag, not a comfort. The holdout is still the judge.

Directional means directional

France and Italy had holdout targeting issues, so their reads are marked directional and scored low confidence. Estimated segments are inferred, not measured. Weight them accordingly.

Real data always wins

When a live experiment contradicts the simulation, the simulation is wrong, full stop. For expensive, one-way decisions, treat this as a prompt to test, never a substitute for evidence.
