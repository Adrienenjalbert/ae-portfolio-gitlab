01 Module

Data Science Principles

Designing a data infrastructure, building a demonstrable artefact, and testing a hypothesis with statistical rigour.

Infra · ETL · statistics

Can front-end analytics stand in for back-end truth?

The brief: design a scalable, GDPR-compliant data infrastructure, then build and evaluate an artefact on real data. I compared milestone events between a web-analytics source (GA4) and the transactional data warehouse to test whether one could proxy the other.

Pearson correlation

An ETL pipeline aligned and anonymised the two sources, then Pearson’s coefficient in Python (SciPy) tested correlation. Revenue-critical events correlated very strongly (r = 0.95, p < 0.001); sign-ups only moderately (r = 0.65), so GA4 is a reliable proxy for some metrics but not all.

MethodsCRISP-DM framing, ETL (extract/transform/load), temporal aggregation for privacy, hypothesis testing (H₀/H₁), scatter + trendline visualisation.
ToolsCloud data warehouse, DBT, SQL, Python (SciPy), Looker Studio, GA4.

04 Module

AI & Digital Innovation

An argued recommendation for a multi-agent architecture, and the three production agents that proved it.

Multi-agent · context engineering

Why hierarchical agents fail, and what to build instead

The brief: critically evaluate an emerging AI innovation and recommend one for the business. I argued that hierarchical “orchestrator-worker” multi-agent systems fail through context degradation, and recommended a decentralised, circular design using the CRM as shared memory, then evidenced it with three deployed agents.

Architecture, earned

The single-agent-with-tools approach drove adoption where hierarchical orchestration had stalled. A later rebuilt enrichment agent scored 93.6/100 against 26.2 for the previous build, the decentralised thesis validated in production.

  • Enablement over automation: a deliberate framing (AI to amplify the team, not replace it) informed by the costs of like-for-like automation.
  • Context engineering over prompt engineering: managing what information reaches which agent, when, with the CRM as the persistent state layer.
  • Three agents shipped into Slack and the CRM: a lead finder/scorer, a signal-report agent and a job-market insights agent.
ToolsRelevance AI, n8n / Make.com, Apollo, Slack, CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) as MCP memory.

05 Work-Based Project · Capstone

Multi-agent AI for account-based sales enablement

The end-point-assessment capstone: a real, pre-gateway work-based project, written up as a 5,000-word report with a presentation and technical questioning, and the same system featured across the portfolio.

Capstone · n8n, Claude API, Salesforce, Slack

From marketing intelligence to adopted sales enablement

Reps spent the majority of their time on manual research against long enterprise cycles. The system qualifies accounts on 20+ signals, finds decision-makers, scores them with an interpretable rubric, and writes back to CRM and Slack, with human approval gates for attribution and GDPR compliance.

What it proved

A “score-then-enrich” waterfall cut enrichment cost ~7×; a CRM-as-memory architecture avoided context degradation; and moving the interface into Slack drove a step-change in adoption. The most valuable system is the one people actually use.

  • CRISP-DM with an added UX validation loop; explicit trade-off analysis on data access, cost-vs-coverage, automation-vs-control and enablement-vs-replacement.
  • Build-vs-buy and centralised-vs-decentralised evaluated against the specific commercial context, with risks and alternatives surveyed.
  • Ethics and governance first-class: data minimisation, bias audits on scoring, interpretable explanations, and a move from web-scraping to structured-API provenance.
