The problem

Organic scale in competitive US markets, cheaply

Growing organic presence across many US cities the manual way would have been prohibitively slow and expensive. The programme needed page-level coverage at a scale hand-built content couldn't reach.

What I built

A programmatic architecture built with coding agents

I designed a programmatic page architecture, the Career Hub, generated with coding agents: a statically rendered Next.js estate of city×role pages built to Core Web Vitals and answer-engine standards. Geo-optimised pages target 50+ US cities. The 4,400-page build was approved by the parent SEO team and is scaling toward 8,800, with 3,000+ pages live as of June 2026.

Around the pages sits the part that compounds: an interactive-tool ecosystem carrying SoftwareApplication and FAQ schema as an AEO moat (articles get summarised by AI Overviews, but interactive tools have to be linked to), an open-source CMS build, and a five-phase self-improving content loop with automated quality scoring across brand, CRAFT, SEO, AEO and depth. The estate improves itself rather than needing a writer per page.
