What these months prove, together

Read as one arc, the monthly updates tell a consistent story across three threads.

Thread 1

AI became standing infrastructure

Chatbots and content engines became a benchmarked, adopted fleet: 16 chatbots shipped, with an AI-first flow converting at 4.7% against a 1.9% form baseline, then a rebuilt enrichment agent scored 3.6× better than the previous one (93.6 vs 26.2) at a 9× lower unit cost, with stakeholder alignment across five teams.

Thread 2

Organic was rebuilt into a compounding asset

A programmatic Career Hub went from a few hundred pages to 2,100+ city×role pages, 51 calculators and 19 tools, all AEO-ready at Lighthouse 98+/100/100, driving +286% clicks and +4,000% impressions year on year while a quality programme raised the bar each month.

Thread 3

Paid scaled at peak without losing efficiency

The 2025 peak grew paid sessions +183% and US active users +94% while UK cost per applied job stayed under £0.25, underpinned by a remarketing read on re-engaged users that became the case for the lifecycle strategy and its 15× long-term retention lift.

Lifecycle

Remarketing unlocks the highest-value audience

A clean read on why lifecycle remarketing matters: re-engaged users convert and stick far better than the rest of the base.

The remarketing lift

Remarketed users convert 6.6× better on applied jobs (16.4% vs 2.5%), run 6.8× more sessions (30 vs 4.4), spend 5.5× longer (1h06 vs 12 min), and are 3× stickier on DAU/MAU. The empirical case behind the 15× long-term retention story.

SOURCE  Google Analytics, RMK vs non-RMK cohort, December 2025.
